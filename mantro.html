Project: Mantro Category: Mobile App

Streamlining your restaurant operations with our intuitive, mobile-friendly platform, with access to essential tools and real-time insights boosting efficiency and productivity.

Objective

Mantro aims to optimize restaurant operations in Tier 2 and Tier 3 cities (India) through a user-friendly, cloud-based platform, providing real-time insights and efficient tools for growth.

Overview

Mantro is a comprehensive hospitality management platform designed to streamline restaurant operations. It provides real-time insights, efficient inventory tools, billing management, and staff coordination features built for restaurants in Tier 2 and Tier 3 cities across India.

Problem Statement

Business Challenges

  • Limited existing solutions tailored for Tier 2/3 city restaurant markets in India
  • High cost of current POS systems makes adoption difficult for small operators
  • Language and literacy barriers in regional markets restrict software usability
  • Unreliable hardware and internet connectivity in semi-urban areas
  • Fear of job displacement among staff due to technology adoption

  • Tailored SolutionsPurpose-built features for the regional restaurant context

Synthesis

Existing solutions either target enterprise clients at premium price points or focus solely on billing without broader operational features. Mantro's key differentiator is its holistic approach — combining inventory, billing, staff management, and real-time analytics in an affordable, offline-ready package purpose-built for India's growing regional restaurant market.

Design Journey

The project began with extensive low-fidelity wireframing tested against real restaurant owners and staff in Tier 2 cities. Early adopters provided invaluable feedback on workflow priorities — billing speed and inventory visibility consistently emerged as the most critical needs.

Leveraging Google Material Design as a foundation allowed rapid iteration without reinventing fundamental UI patterns. This freed the design effort to focus on context-specific flows: table management, kitchen display routing, and multi-role access levels that matched the actual hierarchy of a regional Indian restaurant.

Each round of user testing refined the information architecture. The final design consolidated 70+ screens into a coherent system with consistent navigation, predictable interaction patterns, and a visual language that remained legible across varying device quality and lighting conditions.
